Panther

A black panther is a color variety of the leopard (Panthera pardus) and the jaguar (Panthera tigris) (Panthera onca). Both species of black panthers have an abundance of black pigments, but they also have their characteristic rosettes. Black leopards have been seen in Kenya, India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Thailand, Peninsular Malaysia, and Java, whereas black jaguars have been seen in Mexico, Panama, Costa Rica, and Paraguay. Melanism is caused by a dominant allele in the jaguar and a recessive allele in the leopard.

Iguana

Iguanas are herbivorous lizards found in tropical regions of Mexico, Central America, South America, and the Caribbean. In his book Specimen Medicum, Exhibens Synopsin Reptilium Emendatam con Experimentis circa Venena, Austrian naturalist Josephus Nicolaus Laurenti defined the genus for the first time in 1768. The genus includes two species: the green iguana, which is found across its range and is a popular pet, and the Lesser Antillean iguana, which is found only in the Lesser Antilles.

Tasmanian Devil

The Tasmanian devil belongs to the Dasyuridae family of carnivorous marsupials. It was previously solely found on the island of Tasmania, but a tiny breeding population has been reintroduced to New South Wales in mainland Australia. The disappearance of the thylacine in 1936,the size of a small dog, became the world's largest carnivorous marsupial. It has a quoll-like appearance and is distantly related to the thylacine. It has a large head and neck allow it to generate among the strongest bites.
